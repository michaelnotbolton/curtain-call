# Competitor Analysis — June 2026

## The Three Named Competitors

### GoSeeAShow (goseeashow.com)

**What it is:** A community-driven local theater listing platform with a personal journal feature.

**Strengths:**
- Covers local and small productions (community theater, school shows, youth productions, cabarets, choir concerts with theatrical elements)
- Free personal journal with mood tracking, notes, and a "Journey" stats view (lifetime totals, top venues, top genres)
- Anyone can submit a production — not just theater staff
- No account required to browse
- Genuinely serves the local theater discovery use case

**Gaps:**
- Discovery depends entirely on community submissions — if nobody submits a show, it doesn't appear
- No playwright search
- No actor search
- No map view
- Location search is city/ZIP only, no distance radius
- No advanced filtering beyond location
- No social features (journal is private by default)
- No automated indexing; coverage is uneven across regions

**Positioning vs. Curtain Call:** GoSeeAShow is the closest real competitor. Their community submission model can work where there's an engaged local theater community; it breaks down in markets without active contributors. Curtain Call's automated indexing removes the dependency on submission entirely — shows appear whether or not anyone submitted them.

---

### Showify (showify.us / showify.uk)

**What it is:** Essentially Letterboxd for theater — a social platform for tracking, rating, and sharing theater attendance. UK-origin; US presence at showify.us.

**Strengths:**
- Strong personal tracking: log what you've seen, rate it, write a short review
- Social layer: follow friends, browse public profiles, trending shows
- Watchlist / want-to-see list
- Stats on your own theater-going habits

**Gaps:**
- Not primarily a "find what's playing" tool — it's a tracking app
- No real showtime search or location-based discovery
- No local/small venue coverage
- No playwright or actor search
- No map view

**Positioning vs. Curtain Call:** Showify is in a different lane. It's a social platform for enthusiasts who already know what they're seeing. Curtain Call's personal log competes with the tracking feature, but Curtain Call's main value is discovery. Users who want social validation and a public profile are better served by Showify; users who want to find their next show and record it for themselves are Curtain Call's audience.

---

### StagePass (playbill.app)

**What it is:** A digital playbill companion — you collect digital versions of programs for shows you've attended.

**Strengths:**
- High-quality production detail: cast, crew, songs, production notes
- Offline access to your playbill collection
- Dark mode optimized for in-theater use
- Some local show discovery via location

**Gaps:**
- Discovery is minimal — it's for people who already know what show they're attending
- Not a search tool; not a "what's playing near me" tool
- No playwright or actor search
- Coverage leans toward larger productions where digital playbills are available
- Not a personal log in the journaling sense — it's a collection, not a record

**Positioning vs. Curtain Call:** StagePass is a companion for the night of — you open it when you're already at the theater. Curtain Call is for planning and remembering. These could coexist in a theater fan's phone; they solve adjacent but distinct problems.

## Notable Others

| App/Site | What it does | Gap vs. Curtain Call |
|---|---|---|
| **Playbill** (playbill.com) | Broadway news, cast tracking, personal show diary | Broadway-centric; no local discovery; no automated indexing |
| **Show-Score** | Audience vs. critic ratings; reviews | No showtime search; no local coverage; review-focused |
| **StagePort** | Letterboxd-style log with badges and social sharing | No discovery layer; social-only log |
| **TodayTix** | Ticket purchasing with mood-based filtering | Major shows only; no local coverage; ticket sales focus |
| **BroadwayHD** | Streaming filmed productions | Entirely different product category |
| **On The Stage** | Tools for fringe/indie groups to produce and stream | Venue-facing; not audience-facing |

---

## Market Gap Summary

From the Stage & Cinema roundup of the 10 best theater apps (August 2025):

> "None of these services appear to offer comprehensive playwright search, actor filmography tracking, or small venue/community theater venue indexing as primary functions."

The gap is consistent and documented. Discovery beyond major markets is broken, and no app has automated it.
